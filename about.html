About

GIS Programmer/Analyst

I currently work as a GIS Programmer/Analyst for the Boston Metropolitan Planning Organization's Central Transportation Staff. I develop web applications, conduct spatial analysis, and produce cartography to support regional transportation planning for the Boston Metropolitan Region.

I am interested in environmental and urban planning, and am exploring the uses of GIS and programming to improve transportation access around Boston.

A current project involves using web map APIs to query travel times and flow speeds along corridors in the Boston region. Such open source data can be used to validate transit models and build on the spatial coverage of existing datasets. The working project can be found here.

Planetary Geology

I graduated from Brown University in 2016 with a Sc. B. in Geological Sciences. I completed and received honors for my senior thesis on the extent, diversity, and formation mechanisms of curvilinear ridges in the Nilosyrtis Highlands of Mars. Abstracts on my research were presented at the Lunar and Planetary Science Conference in 2015 and 2016, and a formal paper is in the works.

My research interests are focused on planetary geomorphology, specifically the remote sensing of surficial features on Mars.
